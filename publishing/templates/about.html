About Patient Safety Monitor

Helping healthcare professionals learn from patient safety incidents across the UK, Australia, and New Zealand.

Our Mission

Patient Safety Monitor exists to transform complex coronial findings and patient safety investigations into accessible, educational resources for healthcare practitioners. By systematically collecting and analysing these findings, we help identify patterns, human factors issues, and improvement opportunities that can prevent future patient harm.

Every healthcare professional can benefit from learning what has gone wrong elsewhere. Our goal is to make these learnings easily discoverable and actionable, without requiring practitioners to search through multiple government databases across different jurisdictions.

How It Works

1. Data Collection

We monitor official government sources for new coronial findings and patient safety investigation reports related to healthcare. Our automated systems check these sources regularly to ensure timely coverage of new findings.

2. AI-Powered Analysis

Each finding undergoes analysis using the SEIPS 2.0 (Systems Engineering Initiative for Patient Safety) framework. This structured approach identifies contributing factors across multiple domains:

  • Individual factors: Fatigue, cognitive load, skill level, stress
  • Team factors: Communication, handover, supervision, role clarity
  • Task factors: Complexity, time pressure, interruptions
  • Technology factors: Equipment, usability, maintenance
  • Environment factors: Physical space, resources, crowding
  • Organisational factors: Staffing, policies, culture, training

3. Human Review

All AI-generated content undergoes human review before publication. Our reviewers ensure accuracy, appropriateness, and educational value while maintaining respect for those involved in the original incidents.

4. Publication

Approved summaries are published with clear attribution to original sources, key learnings highlighted for quick reference, and structured human factors analysis for deeper understanding.

SEIPS 2.0 Framework

Our analysis methodology is based on the Systems Engineering Initiative for Patient Safety (SEIPS) model developed by researchers at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. This framework recognises that patient safety outcomes result from complex interactions between people, tasks, technologies, and organisational factors.

Rather than focusing solely on individual errors, SEIPS encourages analysis of the broader work system and how its design contributes to both successes and failures. This systems thinking approach is essential for developing effective, sustainable safety improvements.

Reference: Carayon, P., et al. (2014). SEIPS 2.0: A human factors framework for studying and improving the work of healthcare professionals and patients. Ergonomics, 57(3), 295-307.

Important Disclaimers

  • Educational Purpose Only: Content is intended for educational purposes and should not be used as a substitute for professional medical, legal, or clinical advice.
  • AI-Generated Summaries: While all content undergoes human review, summaries are AI-generated and may not capture all nuances of the original findings.
  • Original Sources: For complete information, always refer to the original coronial finding or investigation report linked in each post.
  • Respect for Individuals: We aim to present findings in a way that respects the dignity of those involved while maximising educational value for patient safety.
